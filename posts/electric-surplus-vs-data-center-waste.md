---
title: Arthur Mench: wasting surplus electricity on data centers is shameful
date: November 2, 2025
excerpt: When plants produce surplus, the real crime is not the low price but drowning that energy in hungry server halls.
---

Arthur Mench is right on one simple point: it is tragic to distribute surplus electricity at low cost to feed data center demand when that energy should serve something else.

We often talk about efficiency and cost per watt. But in practice, the real question is: why is surplus routed to facilities that are not designed to absorb it effectively?

Data centers are massive consumers, yes. But they are not a storage solution. They need constant availability, air conditioning, and redundant networks. Spending excess electricity to keep servers running is not “smart use,” it is poor allocation.

The real shame is that we could use that energy to:

* strengthen local grids in need;
* support decarbonized industries;
* make electricity cheaper for useful peak-time applications.

Instead, part of the energy economy is content to smooth demand by powering server farms. That is not a resilience strategy, it is a way of hiding a lack of vision.

What I take away: buying kilowatt-hours cheaply does not turn a data center into an energy citizen. Surplus deserves a better destination than simply “keeping the lights green.”
