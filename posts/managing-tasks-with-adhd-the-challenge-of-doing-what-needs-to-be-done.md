Living with ADHD, I've found it incredibly challenging to manage everything on my to-do list. My brain works on its own rhythm — it prioritizes tasks based on motivation and the moment rather than deadlines or practical needs.

Despite ambitious personal goals in science, sports, reading, and nutrition, handling basic work responsibilities feels overwhelming. The struggle isn't about exceeding expectations — merely keeping up with them proves difficult.

### Distractions Everywhere

Task sequencing is particularly problematic. Starting one activity often leads to abandonment when something else catches attention. Hours pass with minimal progress on the original list.

### This Week's Struggle

Two pending tasks remain incomplete by Friday afternoon despite one requiring only an hour. With friends arriving at 6 PM, the weekend isn't available for catch-up work. Monday will require explaining the delay to the boss.

### ADHD or Just an Excuse?

There's always a question nagging in the back of my head: does ADHD actually explain the difficulty, or are other factors at play — laziness, avoidance, personal shortcomings? I don't have a clean answer. Probably both, in different proportions on different days.

### Finding a Better Way to Organize My Mind

Progress requires developing a system that works for the way my brain actually functions — not the way it's supposed to function. Organizing the mental backlog, reducing friction on the boring tasks, and finding a structure I can actually maintain rather than one that looks good on paper and collapses by Tuesday.

Still working on it.
