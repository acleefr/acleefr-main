---
title: Retirement: why retirees live better than workers
date: June 14, 2025
excerpt: We talk reform, but France pushes workers toward inactivity while many retirees keep a comfortable life.
---

The real injustice in the French retirement system is not hidden in the pension amount. It shows in how the country tells active workers they must exit the labor market to earn a “decent” life.

I’ve met 55-year-olds still capable of producing, but who already understand their desk will be closed and their contract unplugged before their active life is over.

Meanwhile, retirees enjoy often-paid housing, free time, and a security that no longer exists for many precarious workers. People talk about deficit, but they never discuss how French retirement has become a political tool to reduce labor participation.

The system works like this:

* raise the legal retirement age;
* grant retirement points to those who leave the market;
* make the idea of working after 60 socially shameful.

The result: France pushes people toward inactivity, then celebrates retirees as models of a “good life.” Except this is not a life built on a sustainable career; it is a vulnerability disguised by the security of a system that relies on fewer and fewer active workers.

I’m not saying retirement is useless. I’m saying the balance between retirees and active workers has gone mad: the country rewards leaving work more than it rewards the effort to stay useful.
