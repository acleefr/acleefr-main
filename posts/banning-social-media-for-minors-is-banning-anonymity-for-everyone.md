France just voted to ban social media for under-15s. The stated reason is protecting children. The actual result is the end of online anonymity for every French citizen.

Let me explain why those two things are the same law.

### The Mechanism No One Is Talking About

To enforce an age ban, you need age verification. To verify age at scale, you need every user — not just minors, every user — to prove who they are. That means uploading government ID. Or biometric data. Or both.

The databases that get built from that process link your real identity to your digital activity. Permanently. On every platform you use.

That is not a side effect. That is the system.

### The Security Problem Is Already Here

We are being asked to hand our legal identities to centralized platforms — the same platforms that get breached constantly by sophisticated attacks. The same institutions with professional security teams that still leak millions of records.

Now we want to add government IDs and biometric data to those same targets. And call it child protection.

The risk is not theoretical. Every centralized identity system is a honeypot. Once it exists, it will be attacked. Some of those attacks will succeed. That data does not come back.

### The Paternalism Is the Cover

The framing is always the same: we are doing this for the children. It happened with the health pass. It is happening again here.

The logic is airtight on the surface — who wants to argue against protecting kids? But the mechanism required to enforce the rule has nothing to do with kids. It applies to everyone. It restructures how every adult interacts with every digital service.

Once mandatory identity verification is normalized for social networks, there is no technical or legal barrier to extending it. To forums. To messaging apps. To anything the government decides is sensitive enough to require knowing who you are before you speak.

### Anonymity Was the Last Structural Protection

I am not naive about what anonymity enables. Bad things happen behind it. But it was also the last structural protection against mass surveillance and political censorship for ordinary people — not activists, not dissidents, regular people having regular conversations.

This law removes it. Not as a consequence. As a design choice.

ZK proofs exist. Age can be verified without revealing identity. The technology is real and production-ready. The government is not interested, because the goal is not verification. The goal is identification.

France is building the most complete surveillance infrastructure ever deployed in a Western democracy. It is being handed to actors who have already proven they cannot secure anything. And it is being sold as pediatric hygiene.

I think people deserve to know what they are actually agreeing to.

— Arthur Benjamin Clément
