---
title: AI economy: quick use cases and where humans must stay
date: March 12, 2026
excerpt: AI will create value, but don’t confuse productivity with civilization: humans must keep context, meaning and decision-making.
---

The AI economy is already here. You see it in templates that fill briefs, assistants that complete code, and tools that generate images and summaries.

But we need to separate two things:

* what AI can already do well;
* what AI should not do without a human.

Today, the strongest use cases are those that automate repetitive tasks. Translate a text, generate an email, draft a product page, enrich a database, do quick monitoring. These are time gains, not soul gains.

Where humans must stay:

* the business frame: choosing what to build;
* the meaning: knowing whether a solution is ethical or just efficient;
* responsibility: signing a deliverable, owning a decision;
* domain expertise: understanding what the client really wants when they ask for “more autonomy.”

Discussions about “AI will replace everything” often forget there is an interface economy. As long as models don’t understand the real context of an organization, they remain suggestion engines.

To me, the AI economy is clear: we will replace pieces of work, not people. What we need to build today is an industry where AI makes human work more relevant, not more obsolete.
