---
title: Vibe-Managing Your X Algorithm by Following the Right People
date: June 13, 2026
excerpt: Social networks no longer show posts in the order we expect. To manage your X algorithm, you have to "vibe manage" it by following specific people and engaging with the right content.
---

The old days of a chronological feed are gone. On X, the algorithm decides what you see, when you see it, and which people get pushed into your timeline. If you want your feed to feel curated, you don't just follow people — you follow the right kind of people, in the right order, and you like the content that matches the vibe you want.

### The algorithm is not neutral

X is not presenting posts from people you follow in the order they were shared. It is shaping your attention based on what it thinks will keep you scrolling. That means your feed becomes a reflection of the accounts you follow and the way you interact with them.

If you follow a mix of friends, brands, creators, and random accounts, the algorithm will blend them all together and try to guess what will keep you engaged. The result is noise. The remedy is not more followers — it's a sharper follow list.

### Vibe-manage by following a specific type of person

Some accounts are algorithmically better than others. The ones that make your X feel good are usually:

- consistent in a domain (productivity, design, news, humor, local life)
- the kind of content you actually want to see
- not trying to chase every trend at once

For example, if you want a productive morning feed, follow people who post thoughtful threads, short reads, and quality links. If you want creative inspiration, follow people who share process, tools, and honest work-in-progress posts.

The algorithm notices the people you trust. It also notices when you follow people from the same domain. That creates a stronger signal than following ten random accounts from unrelated niches.

### Like the content you want more of

Following the right accounts is only half the work. The other half is engaging with the content that matches the vibe.

- Like the posts you want to see again
- reply to the voices you want to hear more from
- avoid reacting to noise, even if it is tempting

Every like, reply, and follow is a vote. If you like a deeply personal thread and then like a dozen viral meme posts, the algorithm will keep mixing both. If you only engage with the content that feels intentional, your timeline will lean harder in that direction.

### Treat X like a mood board

Think of your X account as a mood board you can shape. You are not just consuming content — you are tuning a machine.

- Unfollow accounts that pull your feed away from the vibe you want.
- Follow new people when their posts feel like the right frequency.
- pause interactions with content that doesn't fit.

The algorithm will never be perfect. But if you stop expecting X to show you exactly what you followed it for, and start steering it with the people and content you choose, you can make it feel a lot more intentional.

In the end, vibe-managing your X algorithm is less about beating the system and more about building a personal feed that reflects the domains and tone you actually care about.
